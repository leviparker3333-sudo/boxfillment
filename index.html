Core prep categories (detailed)

Amazon‚Äôs prep requirements (updated 2025) are detailed in the FBA service terms. Below are the most common prep scenarios every seller must know:

  • Poly bagging: Any product with exposed fabric, multiple pieces, or that could open during transit must be sealed in a clear, transparent polybag with a suffocation warning (printed on bag or added as a sticker).
  • Bubble wrap / Foam: Glass, ceramics, electronics, or any item marked "fragile" must be individually bubble-wrapped with at least 2 inches of padding on all sides.
  • Labeling (FNSKU): Every unit must have a scannable Amazon barcode (FNSKU) covering any existing manufacturer barcode (EAN/UPC) ‚Äì or have the manufacturer barcode covered with an opaque sticker.
  • Kitting / Bundling: If you sell a ‚Äúset‚Äù (e.g., 3 towels or yoga mat + blocks), they must be securely bundled (shrink wrap or polybag) and labelled as a single unit. Amazon does not pick bundles from separate bins.
  • Expiration dates: Groceries, health, and beauty items with expiration dates must be labelled and sorted with the earliest expiry visible on each case, and sent with at least 90 days of shelf life.
  • Box constraints: Each shipping carton must weigh less than 50 lbs (unless it contains one oversized item) and be properly sealed with reinforced tape.
